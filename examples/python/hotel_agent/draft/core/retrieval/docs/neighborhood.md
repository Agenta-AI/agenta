# Neighborhood Guide

The Agenta Grand Hotel sits on the corner of Linden Avenue and 7th Street, in the **Riverbend** district. Walkable, mostly flat, and well-served by transit. Distances and times below are approximate.

## Transit

- **Linden Avenue Subway Station** — 4 minutes' walk (about two blocks south). Lines 2, 4, and the airport express. Trains every 5–8 minutes during the day, every 15 minutes overnight.
- **Riverbend Bus Hub** — 8 minutes' walk. Local routes plus the cross-town express. Useful for reaching the museum district and the western waterfront.
- **Pellam International Airport** — about **35 minutes by airport-express subway** from Linden Avenue Station, or **25 minutes by taxi/rideshare** outside rush hour. A private car arranged through the concierge runs about $75 one-way.

## Museum and culture

- **The Hartley Museum of Modern Art** — 12 minutes' walk, or one stop on the 2 line. Permanent collection plus rotating exhibitions. Open Tuesday–Sunday, 10:00am–6:00pm; Thursdays until 9:00pm. General admission $22.
- **Riverbend Public Library (Marsh Branch)** — 7 minutes' walk. Architecturally notable; the central reading room is open to visitors. Free.

## Outdoors

- **Greycliff Park** — 10 minutes' walk north. A 40-acre urban park with running paths, a small lake, and seasonal food vendors. Open dawn to dusk.
- **The Riverbend Promenade** — 6 minutes' walk east, runs along the water for about 1.5 miles. Good for strolls, joggers, and street-food carts on weekends.

## Restaurants

- **Calla** — 5 minutes' walk. Modern Italian. Pasta-forward, neighborhood favorite. Reservations recommended; entrées around $26–$38.
- **Sumi & Sons** — 7 minutes' walk. Casual Japanese, well-known for its ramen and small-plate izakaya menu. Walk-ins usually possible; entrées $15–$24.
- **Maison Verte** — 10 minutes' walk. French bistro with a strong wine list, good for a longer dinner. Entrées $30–$45; reservations recommended.
- **The Copper Pot** — 4 minutes' walk. American comfort food, breakfast all day. Cash and card. Entrées $12–$22.

## Shopping

- **Linden Square** — 6 minutes' walk west. A pedestrian street with independent boutiques, a bookstore, two cafés, and a Saturday farmers' market (May through October, 9:00am–2:00pm). Good for an unhurried afternoon.

## Quick orientation

If a guest wants the short version: head south two blocks for the subway, north for the park, east for the river, and west for shops and food. The hotel itself is roughly central to all of those.
