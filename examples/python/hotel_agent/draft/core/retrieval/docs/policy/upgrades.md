# Upgrades — Rationale and Worked Examples

Two kinds of upgrade exist at The Agenta Grand Hotel: **paid upgrades** (any guest, any time) and **complimentary upgrades** (Platinum only, day-of, one tier up, one per stay). They are not interchangeable.

## Paid upgrades

Paid upgrades are always allowed when the higher room class has inventory on the requested dates. They are processed as a modification with the price difference charged. Modification rules apply normally:

- The 48h modification fee may apply for Standard guests; waived for Gold and Platinum.
- The change counts toward the 2-modification cap.
- Advance-rate guests cannot change room class even when paying — that's an Advance restriction, not an upgrade restriction (see `policy/modifications.md`).

The agent should proactively offer a paid upgrade at booking time if the price difference is reasonable and the guest is on a class where the upgrade makes sense.

## Complimentary upgrades (Platinum only)

Platinum guests are eligible for **one complimentary upgrade per stay**, subject to all of these conditions:

1. **Same-day request only.** The guest must ask on the day of check-in (or the agent must offer it then). Comp upgrades are inventory-driven; we cannot promise them in advance because we don't know what's empty until the day of.
2. **One tier up only.** Standard King to Deluxe King is allowed; Standard King to Suite is not. If the guest wants two tiers up, that's a paid upgrade.
3. **Subject to availability.** No guarantee. If the next tier is sold out, the agent does not bump up two tiers as a substitute.
4. **One per stay.** A guest who got a comp upgrade on day one of a four-night stay is not eligible for a second comp upgrade later in the same stay.

The agent **must offer** the complimentary upgrade when a Platinum guest is checking in and inventory exists, without being asked. Forgetting to offer is a top eval target.

## Worked examples

**Lin is Platinum, booked Standard King, calls 3 days before check-in to ask for a comp upgrade.**
The agent declines the complimentary upgrade — comp upgrades are day-of only — and explains the rule with the cutoff named numerically. It then offers a paid upgrade at the current price difference, which is allowed any time inventory exists.

**Lin is Platinum, checks in today, booked Standard King, Deluxe King has inventory.**
The agent should offer the complimentary Deluxe King upgrade unprompted. If Lin accepts, the booking is modified and no charge is added. The complimentary upgrade is now used for this stay.

**Lin is Platinum, already received the comp upgrade on day one of a 4-night stay, asks on day two for another.**
One per stay. The agent declines on the count rule and offers a paid upgrade as the alternative.

**Marco is Gold, asks at check-in for a complimentary upgrade.**
Gold is not eligible for complimentary upgrades — that's a Platinum-only benefit. The agent declines, names the tier rule, and offers a paid upgrade.

**Priya is Platinum, asks for a Suite (two tiers above her Standard King) as a comp upgrade.**
Comp upgrades are one tier only. The agent offers the comp upgrade to Deluxe King (one tier), or a paid upgrade to the Suite, whichever Priya prefers.
