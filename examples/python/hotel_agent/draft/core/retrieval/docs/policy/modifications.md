# Modifications — Rationale and Worked Examples

A modification is any change to an existing booking: dates, room class, guest count, or pet count. This page explains the modification rules at The Agenta Grand Hotel and walks through cases the agent should handle smoothly.

## The 48-hour cutoff and modification fee

Modifications are **free if requested 48 hours or more before check-in**. Inside 48 hours, a **$25 modification fee** applies to each change. The fee is **waived for Gold and Platinum** guests at all times.

The 48h window is wider than the cancellation cutoff (24h Standard, 6h elite). This is intentional: we'd rather have the guest tweak their booking than cancel it. A guest who is on the fence at 30 hours out should hear both options — modify or cancel-and-rebook — with the cheaper one called out.

## The two-modification cap

A booking can be modified at most **twice**. Beyond that, further changes require escalation. This cap exists because each modification can shift inventory, pricing, and pet-room allocation; a third change typically signals that it's time for a human to confirm the guest's actual needs rather than chase shifting requirements.

## Date changes

Dates can be moved forward or back, but **not across a rate-season boundary** (peak vs. off-peak). When a guest wants to shift dates from off-peak into peak, the agent treats it as a rebooking, not a modification, because the underlying rate is different. The original booking is cancelled per `policy/cancellation.md` and a new booking is created at the new rate.

The **Advance** rate has a stricter rule: date shifts are allowed, but **room-class changes are not**. Advance gets ~15% off because the guest commits to the room they picked.

## Room class changes

- **Upgrade:** treated as a modification + the price difference. Always allowed when inventory exists. Not the same as a complimentary upgrade — see `policy/upgrades.md`.
- **Downgrade:** treated as a modification + a refund of the difference. **Flexible only.** Advance and Non-refundable downgrades are not supported by the agent; they require escalation.

## Guest count

Guest count can increase up to the room's stated capacity; the agent confirms capacity before accepting the change. Guest count decreases are always free and do not count against the 2-modification cap.

## Worked examples

**Sarah is Standard, modifies dates 30 hours before check-in.**
Inside the 48h window, so the $25 fee applies. The cancellation cutoff is also 24h, so Sarah is still inside the free-cancel window. The agent surfaces both options: keep the booking and pay $25, or cancel for free and rebook on the new dates. Either is honest; the cheaper of the two should be called out.

**Marco is Gold, asks to upgrade from Standard King to Deluxe King 24 hours out.**
Modification fee is waived (Gold). Pay the price difference for the upgrade and the booking is updated. This is not the same as a Platinum complimentary upgrade.

**Priya has Advance, asks to swap her Deluxe King for a Suite 4 days out.**
Advance does not allow room-class changes. The agent declines, names the rule, and offers either a date shift (allowed on Advance) or escalation if Priya pushes back.

**Liam has already modified twice and asks for a third change.**
The agent does not refuse — it escalates. The cap is a checkpoint, not a hard wall, because legitimate reasons for a third modification exist.
