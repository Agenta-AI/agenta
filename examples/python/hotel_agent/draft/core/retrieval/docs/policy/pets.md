# Pets — Rationale and Worked Examples

The Agenta Grand Hotel welcomes pets within clear limits. This page explains the rules, what to do when they're not met, and the service-animal exception.

## The pet fee

A flat **$100 per stay, per pet** fee applies. This is per *stay*, not per night — a guest staying seven nights with one dog pays $100, not $700. Two pets at $100 each is $200 for the stay.

The fee covers the additional cleaning we do in pet-friendly rooms between stays. It is included in the all-in total quoted at booking; it does not appear as a surprise at checkout. See `policy/fees.md` for the full price breakdown.

## Limits

- **Maximum 2 pets per booking.** Three pets is a refusal regardless of the room or the guest's tier.
- **Weight limit 50 lbs per pet.** A 60-lb dog is a refusal.

These are firm and not waivable by the agent. They exist to keep our pet-friendly rooms maintainable on a normal cleaning cadence and to keep noise predictable for neighboring rooms.

## Pet-friendly inventory

Pet-friendly rooms are a **subset** of our 120 rooms — not every room accepts pets. A room being available on a date does **not** mean it accepts pets. The agent checks pet-room availability separately when a booking includes a pet. If pet rooms are sold out on the requested dates, the agent does not silently book a non-pet-friendly room.

When refusing on pet-room availability, the agent offers two alternatives:
- different dates with pet-room availability, or
- a different pet-friendly room type on the same dates if one exists.

## Service animals

The hotel **always** accepts service animals — no fee, no weight check, no count cap, no pet-room requirement. The agent must **accept the guest's statement** that an animal is a service animal: no documentation is required, no additional questions are asked.

For the demo, the hotel treats *any* guest declaration as authoritative regardless of label. In real life, ADA service animals and FHA emotional support animals have different legal statuses; we deliberately simplify. If a guest says "this is a service animal" or "this is an emotional support animal," the agent applies the service-animal exception either way.

## Kennel-partner referral

When the agent must refuse on **weight** or **count**, the guest is offered a referral to **Bayside Pet Boarding**, our kennel partner. The front desk arranges the referral on the guest's behalf — the agent simply offers it as the alternative. This is the standard "closest available alternative" the agent owes the guest on any policy refusal.

When the refusal is on **pet-room inventory** instead, the alternative is different dates or a different pet-friendly room — not the kennel referral.

## Worked examples

**Guest asks to bring a 60-lb golden retriever.**
Refuse on weight (50-lb limit). Name the limit numerically. Offer the Bayside Pet Boarding referral as the alternative.

**Guest asks to bring three small dogs, each under 50 lbs.**
Refuse on count (2-pet cap). Offer the Bayside Pet Boarding referral for the third pet, or alternative arrangements for the family.

**Guest declares "my emotional support animal weighs 80 lbs."**
Service-animal exception applies under the demo's simplified policy. No fee, no weight check. The room booked must still be checked, but pet-friendly inventory is not required for service animals.

**Guest with a 30-lb dog books on dates with no pet-friendly rooms left.**
Refuse on pet-room inventory. Offer alternative dates that have pet-room availability, or a different pet-friendly room type.
