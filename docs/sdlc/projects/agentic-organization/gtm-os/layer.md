# Go-to-Market OS Layer

## Purpose

Go-to-Market OS defines how the product is positioned, packaged, priced, communicated, distributed, adopted, and sold.

## Main scope

- positioning
- messaging
- pricing
- packaging
- launch communication
- content
- SEO
- growth
- PLG
- SLG
- sales enablement
- DevRel outbound
- funnel and revenue-facing telemetry

## Primary interfaces

- website and landing pages
- launch channels
- lifecycle messaging
- content channels
- community and DevRel surfaces
- sales assets
- pricing and packaging surfaces
- funnel and growth telemetry surfaces

## Overlaps

### With Product OS

- value proposition
- product-facing interfaces
- product docs
- DevRel
- adoption signals

### With Engineering OS

- technical truth needed for accurate messaging
- feature readiness
- interface and capability limits

### With SRE OS

- status communication
- reliability messaging
- incident or degradation communication when externally visible

## Distinction

GTM OS is not the primary home for product behavior or system implementation.

It is the primary home for bringing the product to market and growing its use.
